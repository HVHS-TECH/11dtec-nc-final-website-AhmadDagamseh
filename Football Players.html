Lionel Messi

Lionel Messi has won a record eight Ballon d'Or awards and led Argentina to victory in the 2022 FIFA World Cup. He is the all-time top scorer for both Barcelona and the Argentina national team, racking up over 800 career goals. Additionally, he holds the record for the most trophies in football history, with over 44 collective titles to his name.

Zinedine Zidane

Zinedine Zidane is one of the few figures to reach the pinnacle of football as both a player and a manager, famously winning the 1998 World Cup and the 2002 Champions League on the pitch before leading Real Madrid to an unprecedented three consecutive Champions League titles from the dugout. His individual brilliance earned him the 1998 Ballon d'Or and three FIFA World Player of the Year awards, cementing his legacy as a tactical mastermind and one of the greatest midfielders in the history of the sport.

Cristiano Ronaldo

Cristiano Ronaldo is the all-time leading scorer in mens international football and the UEFA Champions League, holding five winner's medals in the latter. He has won the Ballon d'Or five times and is the only player to have secured league titles and domestic cups in England, Spain, and Italy. As the captain of Portugal, he led his nation to major glory at Euro 2016 and the 2019 UEFA Nations League. Currently playing for Al-Nassr, he has amassed over 900 official career goals and continues to hold the record for the most international appearances in history.

Eden Hazard

Eden Hazard is widely celebrated as one of the greatest wingers of his generation, having won major league titles in France, England, and Spain with Lille, Chelsea, and Real Madrid. His trophy cabinet includes the UEFA Champions League, two UEFA Europa Leagues, and two Premier League titles. Individually, he achieved the rare feat of being named the Ligue 1 Player of the Year twice and later earned the PFA Players' Player of the Year award in 2015.

Ronaldinho

Ronaldinho is a footballing icon who achieved the ultimate "grand slam" of the sport, becoming the only player in history to win the World Cup, Champions League, Copa América, Confederations Cup, Copa Libertadores, and the Ballon d'Or. His peak at FC Barcelona (2003-2008) is credited with rejuvenating the club, leading them to two La Liga titles and their first Champions League trophy in 14 years.

Karim Benzema

Karim Benzema is one of the most decorated strikers in football history, highlighted by his 2022 Ballon d'Or win after a legendary season with Real Madrid where he won both La Liga and the Champions League. He has won a league title with every club he has played for, including four Ligue 1 titles with Lyon, four La Liga titles with Madrid, and a Saudi Pro League title with Al-Ittihad in 2025. Across his career, he has amassed 36 trophies, making him the most decorated French player ever, with his haul including five Champions League titles and a UEFA Nations League trophy with France
